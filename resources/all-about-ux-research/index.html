All About User Research

Are you busy? 4 min read 

What are the real benefits  of User Research ?


Nowadays, User Research is a buzz word but in real what does it means and why is it useful? 


At AllAboutUsers (User Research Agency based in France), we receive a lot of questions. 
Here is a kind of Q and A about what we do and how we do it.  
All about User Research.

1. What is User Research ?

User Research or UX Research definition

User Research or UX Research is an area which aims to understand the expectations of users of a product or service through the whole touchpoints.


User Research should be more focused on Users when UX Research or User Experience Research should be more focused on the experience of User and his/her interactions with the service, product, shop... the touchpoints
 

At AllAboutUsers we do both, the focus depends on the project and the team's questions and needs. 
Simply sometimes, it is both.
Touchpoints are the point of contact between your service/product/brand and your users. They can be mobile/desktop app, website, physical stores, brand image and graphic visuals, product or even packaging.

User Research uses different methods which aim to study the uses and needs of users/customers in order to make better strategic, business decisions for a brand or a company. User research techniques come mainly from the cognitive and human sciences.

At what point should User Research or UX Research to be done?

  • At the beginning of the project
  • When designing the interface, or building the service/the product
  • When the project is already in shape: product, interfaces (mobile, desktop, application, eshop, store)

What about the Frequency ?

User Research or UX Research are important at every stage. They can be launched once or as a strategy at different moments of the brand/company/product life.

For instance, some of our customers launch User Testing sessions every year. 

2. What about the User Research methods?

A customized method

The User Research methods must be customized according to the project, the goals, the users and the needs.

At allaboutusers, we discuss a lot with our clients about their needs, their objectives, the story behind their brand or company. We care about proposing the most appropriated method to have the best results.

Some part of User Research process, we use at AllAboutUsers:

  • Observation, Interviews
  • Shadowing
  • User Testing
  • Focus group
  • UX competitor study or UX Benchmark
  • Website/App Metrics analysis, Hotjar, Data: Clickstream/Click Path
  • Online survey
  • Card Sorting 
  • And many more !

 

3. What about the benefits ?

 

Mainly it helps you to have a better understanding of:

  • your users : who they are, what they like, what they need from your service
  • your service and even sometimes your product. what your product/service bring to your users, how do they use it. We can be surprised to see how some users are using some products.
  • your competitor UX strategy and to improve yours: what is their UX strategy, what impact they have on your users, why what they are doing work and how you can adapt it to your strategy.

 

… to improve your business and take better strategic decisions by :

  • increasing your key rates:  conversion, upsell, sales
  • adapting your service to your users, especially on a specific market as France.
  • Choosing the next features and prioritize
  • Keeping an up-to-date platform

 

4. An example with one of our client's feedback

For instance, one of our customers launched his brand on the french market a few month before we got in touch. Sales were not as good as expected and he wanted to have a better understanding of his/her customers needs/behavior in France (UK brand). 

We launched a User Research study and discovered that some points (words, Presentation on the website, lack of explanation in the buying process, misunderstanding…) were disturbing for the customers. Those points could stop the buying process and bring a mistaken image of the product and the brand pushing the customers to look for a competitor. 

Thanks to the study, we were able to provide our client real and effective tools to help him fulfill his goals based on real feedback and real needs.
